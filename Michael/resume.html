Michael Smith

Oklahoma, United States

In My Youth

I grew up in a small town in western Oklahoma where I worked on my father's farm until I was 16, getting comfortable with diesel engines and high-voltage. I worked in my mother's furniture store, making deliveries and helping customers in the store.

In elementary, I worked with low-code solutions, thanks to Lego. In high school a friend taught me HTML and pushed me to learn JavaScript, while I also worked on computer maintenance and upgrades.

Computer Science at Oklahoma State University

I started working on my undergrad CS degree, where I spent my freshman year working with the Theta Chi fraternity to establish a chapter on campus. Sophemore year I studied Computer Engineering for a semester, where I learned to program FPGA's and the basics of digital logic.

Junior and senior year I was a member of the Information Security and Assurance club, and competed with them in the Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition, winning regionals and competing at nationals. Through this, I had the opportunity to learn server and application security, operations, and cryptography.

Paycom Payroll - NYSE:PAYC

I worked for a year at Paycom working with PHP, MySQL, and JQuery, before I was promoted to team lead over the applicant tracking system. I worked with 5 engineers to train them and add features to our system, learning teamwork, patience, and dealing with a large customer base.

Master's in Management Information Systems

I simultaneously worked on my master's through Oklahoma State University, to get a better understanding of the business-side of my world. My capstone project let me work with 2 other graduates developing a Java-based web application to help the college handle students and their course dependencies: Courses

Stepping down from management at Paycom after 3 years, I moved to a SCRUM team where I focused on React frontend development. We continued innovating the product, including creating a mobile application. I stayed at Paycom for 3 years in this role.

Flogistix

I worked with Flogistix for 6 months where I developed the deployment and frontend for an edge industrial PC that runs on wellsites, using AWS Greengrass, Docker Compose, and React. I learned how to build resilient systems when networks, sensors, and hardware were not always reliable.

Mechstack

I began doing contract work, where I started by working with Oklahoma Robotics. We produced CNC machines for plastic teeth aligners. I set up an instance of LinuxCNC on a Raspberry Pi for each unit, configured the stepper motors, and wired in physical control buttons.

I went on to work with a scuba diving shop to develop an Android and iOS app, an international artist to create her online presence, and a device retailer looking to manage large-scale inventory.
